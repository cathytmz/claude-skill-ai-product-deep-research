# Research Checklist

Use this checklist before drafting and again before final delivery.

## Product Identity

- Official product name and aliases.
- Official website.
- App Store / Google Play / web app / extension / desktop app links.
- Developer account and legal entity.
- Launch date or first visible release date.
- Latest version and update history if relevant.

## Company And Team

- Registered company and jurisdiction.
- Founder, CEO, CTO, product/growth leaders.
- LinkedIn, founder interviews, press releases.
- Funding, investors, acquisitions, accelerator participation.
- Hiring pages and job descriptions that reveal technical stack or market focus.

## Product And UX

- Core user jobs-to-be-done.
- Main onboarding flow.
- Core creation/editing/chat/workflow path.
- Paywall location.
- Free limits, trial, ads, watermark, credits.
- Export/sharing/collaboration path.
- User reviews: praise, complaints, refund issues, quality issues, reliability issues.

## AI And Technical Stack

- Officially disclosed models or providers.
- Observable AI task type: text, image, video, audio, multimodal, agent, search, coding, workflow automation.
- Likely architecture: prompt templates, RAG, vector search, fine-tuning, LoRA, ControlNet, inpainting, segmentation, TTS, ASR, OCR, browser automation, tool use, workflow engine.
- Evidence for each technical inference.
- Compute-cost or latency signals.

## Market And Traction

- Category and segment.
- Market size and growth data.
- Download, revenue, traffic, ranking, review count, rating, retention proxies.
- Regional distribution.
- User segments and willingness to pay.
- Seasonality or viral trend dependency.

## Monetization

- Pricing page and in-app purchase list.
- Weekly/monthly/yearly plans.
- Trial terms.
- Credits, one-time purchases, ads, enterprise/API pricing.
- Refund and cancellation path.
- Dark pattern or compliance signals.

## Competition

- Direct competitors.
- Adjacent substitutes.
- Platform-native alternatives.
- Foundation model/provider alternatives.
- Differentiation and defensibility.

## Growth

- SEO keywords and landing pages.
- TikTok/Instagram/YouTube/X/Reddit/Discord/community presence.
- Influencer or affiliate program.
- Product-led growth loops.
- Templates, public gallery, watermark, share card, invite credits.
- Paid acquisition evidence.

## Risk

- Privacy policy and data retention.
- Biometric, child safety, health, finance, education, employment, or other sensitive data.
- Copyright/training data risk.
- Deepfake or abuse risk.
- Platform policy risk.
- Model/API dependency.
- Regulatory jurisdiction exposure.

## Final Validation

- Every strong factual claim has a source.
- Estimates are labeled as estimates.
- Technical guesses are labeled as inference.
- Latest data dates are included.
- Apify-derived evidence records the original source URL, Actor, collection date, query scope, and limitations.
- Scraped user-generated content is not presented as verified fact.
- Tables are complete enough to be useful.
- The conclusion contains a real strategic judgment, not just a recap.
